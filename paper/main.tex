\begin{abstract}
Physics-informed neural networks are increasingly proposed for bearing fault
diagnosis, but controlled, leakage-free evidence that physics priors actually
\emph{help} is scarce --- and the journal/\allowbreak hydrodynamic-bearing
regime, unlike
the rolling-element datasets that dominate the literature (CWRU, Paderborn), has
almost no public benchmarks. We release \textbf{JBFD-11}, a physics-grounded
\textbf{synthetic} journal-bearing vibration dataset (11 fault classes,
severity-stratified, record-level leakage-checked, with SNR-20/10/5 variants)
from a generator whose spectral signatures are locked by a 34-test CI battery,
together with a \textbf{frozen benchmark of 11 classical, deep, and
physics-informed models, analyzed at the record level}. We use this controlled
testbed to stress-test the physics-informed methods we implement --- a
band-energy spectral-consistency loss judged against a frozen healthy-class
reference, with per-sample operating conditions --- across the regimes where
such priors are theorized to help: noise robustness, data-efficiency, severity
out-of-distribution, interpretability, and calibration. On every axis,
\textbf{the tested physics-informed methods show no statistically supported,
seed-robust advantage} over strong data-driven baselines. We further document
how readily an apparent benefit arises and then disappears under scrutiny: a
promising $n{=}3$ analysis --- a highly significant \emph{representative-seed}
McNemar result ($14$--$0$, $p=1.2\times10^{-4}$) --- \textbf{dissolves under a
pre-registered $n{=}12$ \emph{seed-level} test} (Wilcoxon $p=0.79$); a
pre-registered control that swaps the physics targets for \textbf{random
non-fault frequency bands} has the lowest mean degradation numerically yet still
fails the pre-registered robustness criterion. Our contribution is a reusable
physics-grounded benchmark, a rigorous complete negative on the tested methods,
and a concrete methodological caution about seed counts and estimands in
physics-informed evaluation. We release the dataset, generator, and analysis
code, and \textbf{will} archive the trained checkpoints and full reproducibility
package on a public repository; every headline number was independently
recomputed from the raw checkpoints by separate audit scripts.
\emph{(Synthetic-only; no real-rig validation.)}
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}
Bearing faults are a leading cause of rotating-machinery failure, and
data-driven diagnosis from vibration signals is now standard practice. Two gaps
motivate this work. First, the public benchmarks that underpin the field are
almost entirely \emph{rolling-element} (CWRU~\citep{smith2015cwru},
Paderborn~\citep{lessmeier2016paderborn}); \emph{journal} (hydrodynamic)
bearings --- which fail through different mechanisms, such as oil whirl,
lubrication breakdown, and cavitation, with correspondingly different vibration
signatures --- have almost no openly available labelled fault data. Second,
physics-informed neural networks are increasingly proposed for fault
diagnosis~\citep{raissi2019pinn}, yet controlled, leakage-free evidence that a
physics prior actually \emph{improves} diagnosis --- rather than merely matching
a strong data-driven baseline --- is scarce, partly because real datasets
confound a prior's effect with uncontrolled operating and measurement
variability.

A physics-grounded \emph{synthetic} benchmark addresses both gaps at once.
Because the generator fixes the ground-truth fault, its severity, and the
operating conditions, an evaluation can isolate the contribution of a physics
prior under matched conditions, with leakage controlled by construction. The
cost is external validity --- synthetic signals are not a real rig --- which we
state plainly and treat as the central limitation (\S\ref{sec:limitations}).

We release \textbf{JBFD-11}, a CI-locked synthetic journal-bearing dataset (11
fault classes, severity-stratified, record-level leakage-checked, with SNR
variants) together with a \emph{frozen}, record-level benchmark of 11 classical,
deep, and physics-informed models. We then use this testbed to stress-test the
physics-informed methods we implement --- a band-energy spectral-consistency loss
judged against a frozen healthy-class reference --- across the five regimes where
such priors are theorized to help. The result is a \emph{complete negative}: on
this benchmark, the tested methods show no statistically supported, seed-robust
advantage on any axis. The last candidate positive, a 5\,dB noise-robustness
benefit, illustrates the study's methodological point: it rested on a highly
significant representative-seed record-level McNemar statistic, but
\emph{dissolved} under a pre-registered $n{=}12$ seed-level test
(Fig.~\ref{fig:dissolution}), where it is statistically indistinguishable from a
cross-entropy baseline; a matched-strength non-physics control even had
numerically lower mean degradation, though it too failed the pre-registered bar.

\paragraph{Contributions.}
\begin{itemize}\setlength{\itemsep}{1pt}
  \item \textbf{[C1]} A reusable, CI-locked synthetic journal-bearing dataset and
    generator (11 classes, severity-stratified, record-level leakage-checked, SNR
    variants) --- a controlled testbed for a regime with almost no public
    benchmarks (\S\ref{sec:dataset}).
  \item \textbf{[C2]} A frozen, record-level 11-model benchmark on which no model,
    physics-informed or not, shows an accuracy advantage (\S\ref{sec:benchmark}).
  \item \textbf{[C3]} A rigorous, complete negative on the tested
    physics-informed methods: across noise, data-efficiency, severity-OOD,
    interpretability, and calibration, none gives a statistically supported,
    seed-robust advantage over data-driven baselines (\S\ref{sec:stress}).
  \item \textbf{[C4]} A methodological caution: a representative-seed $n{=}3$
    statistic that appeared significant but dissolves at $n{=}12$ under a
    pre-registered seed-level estimand and a matched-strength non-physics
    control, and a physics loss that passed a green test suite while silently
    broken (\S\ref{sec:caution}).
  \item \textbf{[C5]} A content-hashed, independently re-verified reproducibility
    package; the trained checkpoints will be archived publicly
    (\S\ref{sec:repro}).
\end{itemize}
Throughout, the study is synthetic-only, every estimand is record-level (528) or
seed-level, and the negative is bounded to the methods we implement and test ---
not a claim that no physics prior could ever help.

\section{Related work}
\label{sec:related}
% Citations are deliberately limited to verified, real references already in
% references.bib; expand only with checked entries before camera-ready.
\paragraph{Vibration-based bearing diagnosis.}
Diagnosis from vibration signals has moved from hand-engineered spectral and
statistical features to deep neural networks. The public benchmarks that anchor
this progress are predominantly rolling-element: the Case Western Reserve
University dataset~\citep{smith2015cwru} and the Paderborn
dataset~\citep{lessmeier2016paderborn} are the de facto standards, and most
reported gains are measured on them. Journal/hydrodynamic bearings, whose failure
modes (oil whirl, lubrication breakdown, cavitation) differ from rolling-element
spalling, lack a comparable open benchmark --- the gap JBFD-11 targets.

\paragraph{Physics-informed learning.}
Physics-informed neural networks incorporate governing-equation or domain
knowledge into training, originally for solving differential
equations~\citep{raissi2019pinn} and increasingly as priors or auxiliary losses
for diagnosis and prognostics. The band-energy spectral-consistency loss we test
belongs to this family. Our contribution is not a new prior but a controlled
evaluation --- including a pre-registered $n{=}12$ seed-level noise-robustness
grid --- of whether such a prior helps under matched conditions, the question
that ad-hoc comparisons on confounded real datasets leave open.

\paragraph{Evaluation rigor and reproducibility.}
Several threads in the broader ML literature inform our methodology: attribution
methods such as integrated gradients~\citep{sundararajan2017ig} for the
interpretability axis; calibration measurement via expected calibration
error~\citep{guo2017calibration}; and dataset documentation via
datasheets~\citep{gebru2021datasheets}. To these we add a domain-specific
discipline for physics-informed diagnosis: report seed-level estimands,
pre-register the decision rule, and include matched-strength non-physics
controls, so that a seed-fragile signal is not mistaken for a physics effect.

\section{Dataset \& generator [C1]}
\label{sec:dataset}

\paragraph{Motivation and scope.}
JBFD-11 is a physics-grounded \emph{synthetic} vibration dataset for
hydrodynamic journal-bearing fault diagnosis. Unlike rolling-element bearings,
for which public benchmarks are abundant (CWRU~\citep{smith2015cwru},
Paderborn~\citep{lessmeier2016paderborn}), journal/hydrodynamic bearings have
almost no openly available labelled fault data, yet they fail through distinct
mechanisms with distinct vibration signatures. We therefore generate a
controlled testbed in which the ground-truth fault, its severity, and the
operating conditions are known exactly, so that an evaluation can isolate
\emph{whether a physics prior helps} rather than confounding it with
uncontrolled field variability. The dataset is synthetic throughout; we make no
claim of real-rig fidelity (\S\ref{sec:limitations}).

\paragraph{Signal model.}
Each record is generated as $x(t) = n_{\mathrm{base}}(t) + \sum_\ell
\mathrm{noise}_\ell(t) + s_{\mathrm{fault}}(t)$, sampled at $f_s=20{,}480$\,Hz
for $5$\,s ($102{,}400$ samples) at a nominal shaft speed $\Omega_0=60$\,Hz
($3{,}600$\,RPM). A machine floor $n_{\mathrm{base}}$ and a seven-layer noise
model (measurement, mains EMI, pink $1/f$, thermal drift, ADC quantization,
calibration drift, sporadic impulses, and an occasional aliasing artifact) are
added to a per-class fault signature $s_{\mathrm{fault}}$ scaled by severity and
a transient envelope. Every equation is normative in \texttt{docs/PHYSICS.md}
and locked by a 34-test spectral CI battery
(\texttt{tests/test\_physics\_signatures.py}). The amplitude coefficients are
engineering-plausible values rather than Reynolds-equation solutions: the
\emph{frequency structure}, not absolute g-levels, carries the diagnostic
content.

\paragraph{Classes and signatures.}
The taxonomy comprises eight single faults --- healthy (\texttt{sain}, noise
floor only), misalignment (2X/3X harmonics), imbalance (1X growing with
speed$^2$), clearance/looseness (sub-synchronous $0.43$--$0.48{\times}$ plus
1X/2X), lubrication deficiency (2--5\,Hz stick-slip, amplitude $\propto 1/S$ in
the Sommerfeld number $S$), cavitation (1.4--2.6\,kHz bursts), wear (broadband
floor plus asperity harmonics), and oil whirl ($0.42$--$0.48{\times}$, amplitude
$\propto 1/\sqrt{S}$) --- and three mixed faults that superpose constituents at
reduced amplitude (Fig.~\ref{fig:dataset}). Each signature follows established
vibration-analysis practice, cited per class in \texttt{docs/PHYSICS.md}\,\S4.
Cavitation is only weakly expressed spectrally; we flag this and qualify any
cavitation-specific reading.

  \label{fig:dataset}

\paragraph{Severity, operating conditions, and physics couplings.}
Faults are stratified into four disjoint severity levels (incipient
$0.20$--$0.45$, mild $0.45$--$0.70$, moderate $0.70$--$0.90$, severe
$0.90$--$1.00$) with exactly 80 records per class per level; 30\% of records
carry a linearly progressing severity envelope. Operating conditions are sampled
per record and stored in metadata: shaft speed $\Omega\in[54,66]$\,Hz
($\pm 10\%$), load 30--100\% of rated, and temperature 40--80$^\circ$C, from
which an oil viscosity $\mu(T)=\mu_{\mathrm{ref}}e^{-0.03(T-60)}$ and a
Sommerfeld number
$S=S_{\mathrm{base}}\,(\mu/\mu_{\mathrm{ref}})(\Omega/\Omega_0)/\mathrm{load}$
(clipped to $[0.05,0.50]$) drive the lubrication and oil-whirl couplings. The
recorded per-record conditions provide the per-sample operating-condition inputs
used by the physics-informed method (\S\ref{sec:stress}).

\paragraph{Windows, splits, and leakage control.}
Records are split at the \emph{record} level ($70/15/15$), stratified by
class$\times$severity, into $2{,}464 / 528 / 528$ records; training and
evaluation use 1\,s windows ($20{,}480$ samples, five non-overlapping windows
per record) cut at load time by a group-aware \texttt{WindowedView} that never
crosses a record boundary, giving $12{,}320 / 2{,}640 / 2{,}640$ windows. Because
all five windows of a record share its fault, severity, operating point, and
noise realization, the windows are \emph{not} independent --- a fact that
governs the statistical unit throughout (\S\ref{sec:benchmark}). Splits are
leakage-checked by record hash at generation, and both independent audits
re-verified zero cross-split overlap by content-hashing the released file. For
noise-robustness evaluation the \emph{test} split is additionally exported at
three SNR levels ($20/10/5$\,dB AWGN, calibrated to each record's clean RMS), so
frozen checkpoints are evaluated across noise without retraining.

\paragraph{Release and datasheet.}
We release the generator, the dataset
(\texttt{data/generated/dataset\_v2.h5}; SHA-256 pinned in
\texttt{results/PROVENANCE\_MANIFEST.md}), and a datasheet following
\citet{gebru2021datasheets} (Table~\ref{tab:datasheet}), under CC-BY-4.0.
Generation is deterministic (global seed 42; per-record seed $42+i$).
\S\ref{sec:repro} details the full reproducibility chain.

\section{Benchmark \& protocol [C2]}
\label{sec:benchmark}

\paragraph{Protocol (frozen in advance).}
The benchmark task is 11-class fault classification on 1\,s windows. The
protocol was frozen before running (\texttt{experiments/PROTOCOL.md}): every deep
model is trained with an identical budget (Adam, $\mathrm{lr}=10^{-3}$; batch
64; $\le$60 epochs; early stopping with patience 10 on validation accuracy; no
learning-rate schedule; cross-entropy loss; no augmentation); model selection
uses the validation split only; and the clean \texttt{test} split and its
\texttt{test\_snr*} variants are each touched exactly once, after training. We
evaluate eleven models: eight deep networks (\texttt{cnn1d},
\texttt{attention\_cnn}, \texttt{cnn\_lstm}, \texttt{resnet18},
\texttt{patchtst}, \texttt{hybrid\_pinn}, \texttt{physics\_constrained\_cnn},
\texttt{multitask\_pinn}), three classical baselines (random forest, SVM,
gradient boosting on a 36-feature extraction), and a soft-voting ensemble; each
at three seeds.

\paragraph{The record is the inferential unit.}
The $2{,}640$ test windows derive from 528 records, five per record, and share
within-record structure; treating them as independent overstates significance
(an early error, caught by audit and corrected). We therefore aggregate each
record by soft vote (mean class score over its five windows) and compute all
statistics on the \textbf{528 records} as the independent unit. We report this
both because it is the correct estimand here and because the same
window-versus-record distinction recurs, at the seed level, in the stress-test
(\S\ref{sec:stress}).

\paragraph{Results: near-ceiling, no accuracy advantage.}
At the record level the task is near-ceiling (Table~\ref{tab:benchmark}): the
strongest classical baseline is random forest ($98.74\%$) and the strongest deep
model is \texttt{cnn\_lstm} ($99.43\%$). No model --- physics-labelled or not ---
shows an accuracy advantage. The best vanilla deep model (\texttt{cnn\_lstm},
best-validation seed $99.62\%$) and the best physics-labelled model
(\texttt{physics\_constrained\_cnn}, $99.62\%$) are tied: the paired gap is
$+0.00$ points ($95\%$ CI $[+0.00,+0.00]$, cluster bootstrap by record) with an
exact McNemar $p=1$.

\paragraph{Honest relabelling.}
The physics-labelled rows do not test physics-informed \emph{training}, and are
labelled accordingly. \texttt{physics\_constrained\_cnn} here is CE-only: its
physics term is a separate loss that the benchmark training loop never invokes,
so the row is architecture-only with the physics loss OFF.
\texttt{hybrid\_pinn} carries a rolling-element (not journal-bearing) physics
branch plus constant metadata, and \texttt{multitask\_pinn} runs single-task
with its auxiliary heads unused. The benchmark is thus a clean
\emph{classification} benchmark on which physics architecture confers no
accuracy edge; the actual test of physics-informed \emph{learning} is the
stress-test of \S\ref{sec:stress}.

\section{Stress-testing the tested physics-informed methods [C3]}
\label{sec:stress}
\paragraph{The tested method.}
The physics-informed method we test is a physics-constrained CNN (PC-CNN;
\code{physics_constrained_cnn} in the release): a ResNet1D backbone whose physics
enters through an additive loss term weighted by $w$. The term is a
\emph{band-energy spectral-consistency} loss judged against a \emph{frozen
healthy-class reference}. For each class $c$ and each of its expected frequency
bands $b$ --- journal-bearing tonal harmonics built from the \emph{per-sample}
shaft speed plus absolute broadband bands (\code{docs/PHYSICS.md}, \S4) --- the
band penalty is
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{pen}_b \;=\; \mathrm{relu}\!\left(1 - \frac{\mathrm{frac}_b}{H_{\mathrm{ref}}[c][b]}\right),
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{frac}_b$ is the signal's energy fraction in band $b$ and
$H_{\mathrm{ref}}$ is the measured healthy-class per-band energy fraction
(\code{healthy_reference.json}, from 1{,}120 training healthy windows).
Per-class penalties are averaged over the class's bands and weighted by the
softmax probability of $c$. The loss is differentiable and unit-tested, and the
per-sample operating conditions are wired into training. We stress-test this one
method across the five regimes where physics priors are theorized to help: noise
robustness, data-efficiency, severity out-of-distribution, interpretability, and
calibration.

\paragraph{The pre-registered $n{=}12$ grid (centerpiece).}
Noise robustness is the regime where a candidate positive last survived, so we
designed a pre-registered grid to stress-test it. The protocol --- four arms,
the seed-level estimand, and the decision rule --- was committed
(\texttt{experiments/PROTOCOL.md}\,\S8.8, git \texttt{4a2063d})
\emph{before} the runs; all 48 \texttt{metrics.json} carry that commit and
finished after it. All four arms share the same PC-CNN architecture, budget, and
dataset, at 12 seeds: (i)~\textbf{CE-only} ($w{=}0$), retrained in the same code
path; (ii)~\textbf{correct} band-energy ($w{=}1.0$), the validated loss;
(iii)~\textbf{scramble} ($w{=}1.0$), each fault judged against a \emph{different}
fault's real bands (a fixed seeded derangement) --- same loss strength, wrong
assignment; and (iv)~\textbf{random-band} ($w{=}1.0$), each class judged against
\emph{random non-fault} bands of matched count and width, provably
non-overlapping with any real characteristic band (frozen
\texttt{random\_reference.json}) --- a matched-strength \emph{non-physics}
control. The estimand is the paired per-seed clean$\rightarrow$5\,dB
record-level degradation, tested by a seed-level Wilcoxon signed-rank against
CE-only; the fixed decision rule sets $\tau=1.0$\,pt and calls an arm
``robust'' only if its degradation is below $\tau$ on $\ge$10/12 seeds
\emph{and} its Wilcoxon $p<0.05$.

\paragraph{Result: the candidate positive does not replicate.}
At $n{=}12$ no arm clears the bar (Table~\ref{tab:grid},
Fig.~\ref{fig:spread}). Correct physics degrades $3.47\pm3.42$\,pt versus
CE-only's $3.54\pm3.85$ --- statistically indistinguishable (seed-level Wilcoxon
$p=0.79$) --- and is robust on only 5/12 seeds; scramble is $4.89$\,pt
($p=0.75$, 7/12) and random-band is $2.15$\,pt ($p=0.21$, 9/12). Every arm is
dominated by seed variance, and the direction of the (non-significant) effects
is itself telling: the \emph{non-physical} random-band control has the lowest
mean degradation and the highest robust-seed count, while \emph{correct} physics
is the weakest of the three $w{=}1.0$ arms. The data therefore support neither a
physics-specific benefit nor a generic spectral-regularizer benefit; the
candidate positive was seed-fragile (\S\ref{sec:caution}).

\paragraph{The other four regimes are each negative.}
Across the remaining regimes the tested method shows no advantage either, all at
the record level (Table~\ref{tab:regimes}). \emph{Data-efficiency} (\S8.2):
PC-CNN exceeds the best vanilla model (\texttt{resnet18}) with non-overlapping
$\pm1$\,std at only 1 of 3 reduced training fractions --- failing the
pre-registered rule of $\ge$2 of 3 --- and ties at 10\% and 50\%.
\emph{Severity-OOD} (\S8.3): training on low severity and testing on severe ties
at ceiling (both $100\%$); the harder reverse direction favours PC-CNN on the
point estimate ($82.58$ vs $76.01$) but with McNemar $p=0.39$ and a gap CI of
$[-2.27,+8.33]$ spanning zero --- direction-only, not a positive.
\emph{Interpretability} (\S8.6a): the integrated-gradients attribution fraction
in the physics bands \emph{reverses} --- the vanilla model places more energy
in-band ($0.146$) than the physics model ($0.099$), and on
\texttt{lubrification} both place essentially zero --- giving no evidence of a
physics-attention mechanism. \emph{Calibration} (\S8.6b): both models are
well-calibrated (ECE $<0.03$) and the 5\,dB ECE direction flips between
MC-dropout runs --- a wash. In sum, the implemented band-energy
spectral-consistency method shows no statistically supported, seed-robust
advantage on any tested axis; this negative is bounded to that method (one
family), not a claim about physics priors in general
(\S\ref{sec:limitations}).

\section{Methodological caution [C4]}
\label{sec:caution}
\paragraph{A representative-seed $n{=}3$ signal dissolves at $n{=}12$.}
The clearest lesson of this study is how readily an apparent physics benefit
arises and then disappears under a stricter estimand
(Fig.~\ref{fig:dissolution}). The same $n{=}12$ grid above reproduces the
earlier $n{=}3$ ``win'' exactly on its first three seeds --- seeds
$\{0,1,2\}$ give CE-only degradation $4.29$\,pt versus correct physics
$0.06$\,pt --- and the remaining nine seeds erase it, leaving the seed-level
null ($3.54$ vs $3.47$\,pt, Wilcoxon $p=0.79$). The record-level
representative-seed McNemar that made the $n{=}3$ result look decisive
($14$--$0$, $p=1.2\times10^{-4}$) is the \emph{confounded} statistic: it
conditions on a single representative seed and cannot estimate cross-seed
variance. We report it only as the statistic that fooled the $n{=}3$ analysis,
never as evidence of an effect; the pre-registered seed-level test governs.

\begin{figure}[t]
  \centering
  \figfromfile{F2_n3_to_n12_dissolution.pdf}
  \caption{\textbf{Headline.} The apparent noise-robustness benefit is a seed
    artifact. \emph{Left}: at $n{=}3$ (the grid's seeds 0--2) the
    correct-physics arm appears far more robust than CE-only (mean degradation
    $0.06$ vs $4.29$\,pt). \emph{Right}: the same comparison under the
    pre-registered $n{=}12$ seed-level test is a null ($3.47$ vs $3.54$\,pt,
    Wilcoxon $p=0.79$). Same data, same code path; the apparent gap dissolves as

  \label{fig:dissolution}

\paragraph{A physics loss can pass a green test suite while silently broken.}
The corrected loss above was reached only after five successive defects, each of
which passed a green test suite. In order: (1)~the physics term was never
invoked by the benchmark training loop, so the ``physics'' model was trained
CE-only; (2)~once invoked, it was non-differentiable (an \texttt{argmax} gave
zero gradient, making the weight inert --- a byte-identical weight sweep);
(3)~the signature database encoded \emph{rolling-element} fault frequencies
(BPFO/BPFI), the wrong bearing type, with the mixed classes unmapped;
(4)~rebuilt for journal bearings, it judged band energy against a \emph{flat}
spectrum, letting shared healthy energy masquerade as a fault; and
(5)~corrected to judge against the frozen healthy-class reference. A model named
\texttt{physics\_*} proves nothing: tests must assert gradient flow,
database-to-data consistency, and the correct baseline --- not merely that the
code runs.

\paragraph{Recommendations.}
For physics-informed evaluations of this kind we recommend: (i)~use the
\emph{seed} as the inferential unit, not the window, and report enough seeds to
estimate seed variance; (ii)~\emph{pre-register} the seed-level estimand and the
decision rule before running; and (iii)~include both a \emph{scrambled}-physics
control and a \emph{matched-strength non-physics} control (here, random
non-fault bands) so that an apparent effect can be separated from any
regularizer of equal strength. Because even the numerically best non-physics
control failed the pre-registered bar, the grid supports neither a
physics-specific nor a generic spectral-regularizer benefit.

\section{Limitations \& scope}
\label{sec:limitations}
\paragraph{Synthetic only --- the central caveat.}
JBFD-11 is synthetic. Its amplitude coefficients are engineering-plausible
values, not Reynolds-equation solutions, and the signals carry no
housing/sensor transfer path; the \emph{frequency structure} is physically
grounded but absolute fidelity to a real test rig has not been validated. The
dataset's value is controlled ground truth, not field realism, and no result
here should be read as a real-bearing or field claim. Bridging to real rigs is
future work.

\paragraph{Near-ceiling clean task.}
The clean classification task is near-ceiling (\S\ref{sec:benchmark}), so the
benchmark's message is \emph{relative} --- physics architecture adds nothing ---
rather than an absolute accuracy claim. A near-ceiling task also limits the
headroom in which some advantages could appear; we therefore concentrated the
physics test on regimes with headroom (noise, reduced data, severity shift),
pre-registered a seed-level estimand, and added a matched-strength non-physics
control --- none of which surfaced an advantage.

\paragraph{Scope of the negative.}
The negative is bounded to the \emph{methods we implemented and tested} --- one
family, a band-energy spectral-consistency loss judged against a healthy-class
reference, together with the benchmarked physics-labelled architectures --- on a
single synthetic dataset. It is \textbf{not} a claim that no physics prior could
ever help journal-bearing diagnosis: a different prior, a different coupling, or
a real-data regime could behave differently. We exclude the rolling-element
HybridPINN branch as out of scope (wrong bearing type), and we note that
cavitation is only weakly expressed spectrally, so cavitation-specific readings
are qualified.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We introduced JBFD-11, a physics-grounded synthetic journal-bearing dataset and
a frozen, record-level benchmark, and used them to stress-test a band-energy
physics-informed method across five regimes. On this controlled testbed the
tested method shows no statistically supported, seed-robust advantage over strong
data-driven baselines on any axis, and the one candidate positive dissolved once
the seed --- not the window or a single representative seed --- was made the
inferential unit at $n{=}12$. We report this as a negative result and a
methodological caution, not as evidence against physics priors in general: the
finding is bounded to the implemented methods on a single synthetic dataset. The
lasting contributions are the reusable benchmark and the evaluation discipline it
makes concrete --- record-level and seed-level estimands, pre-registered decision
rules, and matched-strength non-physics controls --- which we hope are useful for
assessing physics-informed diagnosis as real journal-bearing data become
available. We release the dataset, generator, and analysis code, and will archive
the trained checkpoints and full reproducibility package publicly.
